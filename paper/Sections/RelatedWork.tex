\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

The mechanism sits at the intersection of three literatures, following the framing of
\textcite{srinivasan2025selfresolving}.

\paragraph{Agreement and aggregation.}
\textcite{aumann1976agreeing} showed that common knowledge of posteriors stops common-prior
Bayesians from ``agreeing to disagree''; later work~\parencite{kong2022aggregation} shows
that repeated belief exchange reaches consensus, and when signals are \emph{informational
substitutes}~\parencite{chen2016informational} that consensus aggregates all private
information. This explains \emph{how} honest agents
pool information but assumes honesty; it gives no incentive to reveal a signal. The
self-resolving market supplies exactly those incentives on top of the Aumannian protocol.

\paragraph{Prediction markets and market scoring rules.}
With an observed outcome, truthful elicitation is solved: proper scoring rules reward
matching the realised outcome~\parencite{brier1950verification,good1952rational,gneiting2007strictly}.
\textcite{hanson2003combinatorial} extended these to \emph{market scoring rules}, which pay
a sequence of agents for how much each \emph{improves} a shared forecast;
\textcite{chen2012designing} relate this to automated market makers and
\textcite{chen2010gaming} to equilibrium play. All ultimately pay against an outcome. The
self-resolving market keeps the cross-entropy market scoring rule but swaps the outcome for
a reference agent's report, the move we test.

\paragraph{Elicitation without verification, and where we fit.}
Peer prediction~\parencite{miller2005eliciting}, the Bayesian truth
serum~\parencite{prelec2004bayesian}, output agreement~\parencite{waggoner2014output}, and
information-theoretic designs~\parencite{kong2019information} incentivise truth-telling with
no outcome, but mostly target elicitation rather than aggregation and admit uninformative
equilibria.
The self-resolving market is a peer-prediction mechanism engineered, via informational
smallness, to \emph{also} aggregate efficiently and pay near-zero in uninformative
equilibria. Crucially, \textcite{srinivasan2025selfresolving} prove their guarantees but
provide no empirical or simulation evidence; our contribution is to fill that gap,
checking in simulation whether the incentive story plays out when an agent may deviate,
and on real market data whether the reference-based payouts track the outcome-based payouts
they replace.
