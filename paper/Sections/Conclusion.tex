\section{Conclusion, Discussion, and Limitations}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We gave empirical content to the self-resolving prediction market of
\textcite{srinivasan2025selfresolving}. In a synthetic world where its assumptions hold
(RQ1), a freely deviating agent's profit-maximising report converges onto truthful
reporting as the reference accumulates informational substitutes ($\gamma^\star\to 1$ by
$k\approx 64$), operationalising Theorems~1 and~5; we also reproduced the analytic $k_{\min}$
bound, confirmed exact aggregation under truthful play, and showed the mechanism degrades
gracefully when agents are boundedly rational (A1) or the common prior is mis-specified (A2).
On roughly
$90$ resolved
Polymarket markets (RQ2) the self-resolving payouts track the verifiable payouts almost
perfectly at a late reference and degrade gracefully as it moves earlier, isolating outcome
leakage as the residual channel.

The two studies answer genuinely different questions (RQ1 about \emph{incentives} where
the assumptions hold, RQ2 about \emph{outputs} where they fail) and together suggest the
mechanism is more robust in practice than its worst-case theory promises (truthfulness
onsets at $k\approx 64$, well inside the $k_{\min}\approx 146$ bound). Notably, RQ2's
residual error lives almost entirely in the \emph{ranking} of who deserves reward, not in
payout \emph{levels}: a designer can recover the right total spend and reward roughly the
right behaviour at an early reference, but not the fine-grained ordering without leaking the
outcome.

\paragraph{Limitations and future work.}
RQ2 measures outputs, not incentives (we replay fixed paths and never let agents deviate),
and real markets violate essentially all of A1--A5, so the agreement is evidence of
\emph{robustness}, not of the assumptions holding. The late reference also conflates
``well-informed'' with ``outcome-contaminated''; the synthetic study breaks A1 and A2 but
holds A3 and A4 fixed, so correlated signals (which would violate A4) remain the most
important untested case; and everything is restricted to binary outcomes. Two directions follow: can the
CE-MSR be reweighted so the \emph{ranking} of rewards tracks who pushed price toward truth
\emph{without} observing the outcome, and does the reference-as-proxy idea extend to
non-binary outcomes and to mild violations of conditional independence? The gap between the
loose $k_{\min}$ and the much earlier empirical onset also suggests room for tighter,
prior-dependent guarantees.
